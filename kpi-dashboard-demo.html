Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Trend Monthly CAC vs Target
Understanding Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):

CAC represents the maximum amount you can spend to acquire a customer while maintaining your target 10% net margin. This metric is critical for profitable growth.

  • Target CAC (~$61): Based on your unit economics and 10% margin requirement
  • Seasonal Patterns: CAC typically rises in Q1 (budget resets) and falls in Q4 (year-end urgency)
  • Optimization Opportunity: Every $10 reduction in CAC = significant additional annual profit at your volume

Key Insight: Your CAC has improved since Q1, indicating better marketing efficiency and sales conversion.

Contribution Margin Analysis $ Value & %
What is Contribution Margin?

Contribution Margin is the revenue remaining after variable costs (COGS, fulfillment) but before marketing spend. It shows how much each sale contributes to covering fixed costs and profit.

  • Current Rate: For every $370 order, strong margins cover fixed costs and profit
  • Industry Benchmark: Kitchen sink and home improvement products typically range 35-50%
  • Improvement Impact: Each 1% improvement = meaningful additional annual margin

Strategic Value: Your strong contribution margin provides flexibility for competitive pricing or increased marketing investment.

Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) Monthly Performance
ROAS Explained:

Return on Ad Spend measures revenue generated for every marketing dollar spent. Your target of ~5.8x means generating $5.80 in revenue per $1 of marketing spend.

  • Current Performance (~5.8x): Strong performance for sustainable profitability
  • Relationship to CAC: ROAS = AOV ÷ CAC (inverse relationship)
  • Optimization Path: Improving conversion rate by 0.5% increases ROAS further

Action Item: Focus on channels consistently delivering >6x ROAS while testing new channels at 5x minimum.

LTV:CAC Ratio Trend Unit Economics Health
The Golden Metric - LTV:CAC Ratio:

This ratio compares Customer Lifetime Value to Customer Acquisition Cost, indicating the overall health of your unit economics.

  • Your Ratio (~8.4x): Exceptional - you generate $8.40 in value for every $1 spent acquiring customers
  • Industry Standards:
    • < 3x: Unsustainable
    • 3-5x: Healthy
    • > 5x: Excellent (your current position)
  • Components: LTV (~$518) ÷ CAC (~$61) = ~8.4x

Strategic Implication: Your exceptional ratio suggests strong room for accelerated growth through increased marketing investment.

Cost Per Lead - The Top of Funnel Metric:

CPL measures the efficiency of your lead generation efforts. It's the first step in the CAC equation: CPL × (1 ÷ Conversion Rate) = CAC.

  • Current CPL: Shows improved targeting efficiency
  • Relationship to CAC: At 4.2% conversion, CPL directly impacts your target CAC of ~$61
  • Channel Mix Impact: Different channels have varying CPLs - optimize for quality, not just cost

Pro Tip: Reducing CPL while maintaining quality provides significant annual savings at your lead volume.

Lead Conversion Rate Trend Lead → Order Efficiency
Conversion Rate - The Efficiency Multiplier:

Lead-to-order conversion rate is the most powerful lever for reducing CAC. Small improvements create exponential impact.

  • Your Rate (4.2%): Significantly above 2-3% industry average for home improvement products
  • Impact Analysis:
    • 0.5% improvement → significant CAC reduction
    • 1% improvement → substantial CAC reduction
  • Optimization Levers: Lead scoring, nurture sequences, sales enablement

Key Insight: Your above-average conversion suggests strong product-market fit and effective sales process.
